The alignment problem

Staff, teams, and departments are constantly out of sync.

The systems are there — PMS, booking platforms, messaging tools. But they were never built to help people coordinate. The systems are outdated and fragmented — they don't talk to each other, and they were never designed to be sufficient on their own. That leaves staff to work around the systems and fill in the gaps themselves. Right now that means heavy reliance on memory, Post-its, spreadsheets, and physically chasing each other and information around the hotel.

The real operating system

Memory and paper is the real operating system.

Human intelligence is what keeps the outdated systems running. The tools don't communicate with each other and aren't sufficient on their own — so staff fill the gaps with memory, habit, and presence. And every time a shift changes, that knowledge walks out the door.

The consequence

Promises get forgotten.
Guests notice.

When staff spend their time searching for information instead of being present, the guest experience suffers. Not because anyone failed — because the system was never built to support them.

When the systems fail the staff,
the guest pays the price.

When staffspend their shifts chasing updates, filling in system gaps, and relying on memory to do a job the tools were supposed to support. They know what a great guest experience looks like — and they want to deliver it. The systems hold them back.

That exhaustion surfaces exactly where it hurts most. Missed promises. Repeated requests. Managers absorbed in coordination failures instead of leading their teams. Every gap in the staff's information becomes a gap in service — and every gap in service becomes a cost in loyalty, reputation, and revenue.

The guest doesn't see the fragmentation.
They feel the result of it.

What our interviews revealed
"I want to stay continuously aligned across shifts, departments, and guest situations — so I can carry out the right task, at the right time, with the right information, without manually chasing updates, people, or priorities."
Our design question

How might we help hospitality staff manage tasks, communication, and the guest experience more efficiently — while preserving the human touch?

↓
The answer
Mise

An AI agent that replaces the Post-its, the memory, and the manual coordination holding hotel operations together — keeping staff aligned across shifts, departments, and guest situations.

Not replacing existing systems.
The intelligent layer between them and the people who use them.

Action happens here
01 — Task management

Every request,
one place.

From any channel. The agent picks it up, classifies it, assigns it, and tracks it. Every task is connected to a guest — not just a to-do item.

02 — Guest profile

The operational
brain.

Preferences, history, dietary needs, complaints, promises. Every priority decision flows from who the guest is and what they need right now.

03 — Shift handover

Knowledge that
never leaves.

When a shift ends, the next team is automatically briefed. Open tasks, guest situations, promises made. Nothing is lost when someone goes home.

04 — Managerial control

Oversight without
the bottleneck.

Live task board, workload visibility, override controls. Managers write plain-language instructions that shape how the agent thinks — and it learns from every decision.

05 — Chat

Ask anything.
Get context.

Plain-language access to every guest, task, and team update. No digging through systems — Mise surfaces what you need, exactly when you need it.

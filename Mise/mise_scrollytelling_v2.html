Hotels run on
people,
not systems.

The systems are there. But they don't work together — and the people fill the gaps.

Scroll
The problem

The systems don't talk
to each other.

PMS, booking platforms, communication tools, housekeeping software — each one is an island. Outdated, disconnected, never designed to work as one.

The workaround

So the people
fill the gaps.

Memory. Post-its. Spreadsheets. Physically chasing each other around the hotel to get anything done. Human intelligence is the only thing holding the operation together.

The result

Every shift change is a
moment knowledge dies.

The person who made a promise to a guest is gone. The note is on a Post-it no one will find. The next shift starts from zero.

That has a cost
on both sides.

For staff

Time spent chasing information, filling gaps, and holding fragmented systems together is time taken away from guests. That is not why anyone went into hospitality.

For the business

Missed promises, repeated errors, managers pulled into emergencies they should never handle. Every incident is a cost that never makes it onto a dashboard.

That cost is invisible internally.
The guest feels it.

What staff actually want
"To stay continuously aligned across shifts, departments, and guest situations — so I can carry out the right task, at the right time, with the right information, without manually chasing updates, people, or priorities."
Our design question

How might we help hospitality staff manage tasks, communication, and the guest experience more efficiently — while preserving the human touch?

↓
The answer
Mise

An AI agent that replaces the Post-its, the memory, and the manual coordination holding hotel operations together. The intelligent layer between systems and the people who use them.

Not replacing existing systems.
Sitting above them.

Action happens here
01 — Task management

Every request,
one place.

From any channel — guest messages, staff notes, system alerts. The agent picks it up, classifies it, assigns it, and tracks it. Every task is connected to a guest, not just a to-do list.

02 — Guest profile

The operational
brain.

Preferences, dietary needs, complaint history, past stays, promises made. Every priority decision the agent makes flows from who the guest is and what they need right now.

03 — Shift handover

Knowledge that
never leaves.

When a shift ends, the agent automatically briefs the next team. Open tasks, guest situations, promises made. Nothing is lost when someone goes home.

04 — Managerial control

Oversight without
becoming the bottleneck.

Live task board, workload visibility, override controls. Managers write plain-language instructions that shape how the agent thinks — and the agent learns from every decision.
